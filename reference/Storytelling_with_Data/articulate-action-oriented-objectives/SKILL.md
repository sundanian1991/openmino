# Articulate Action-Oriented Objectives

## When to Use
Use this skill when:
- Communicating data findings to stakeholders (business professionals, analysts, or managers)
- You have defined your audience and derived actionable insights from data
- The goal is to prompt a decision, behavior change, or focused discussion—not just share information
- The document is meant to influence action (not archival/reference material)

## How to Execute
1. **Define the desired action**: State precisely what the audience should do or decide after engaging with your communication (e.g., “approve $50K budget,” “revise customer onboarding process”).
2. **Frame appropriately**: If a direct recommendation feels premature or inappropriate, propose open-ended but action-catalyzing prompts (e.g., “Should we pilot this solution in Q3?”).
3. **Use strong action verbs**: Structure your objective using decisive verbs such as *implement*, *recommend*, *validate*, *support*, *adjust*, or *prioritize*.
4. **Force a decision point**: Avoid conclusions that invite passive acknowledgment; instead, create a clear fork in the road requiring a response.
5. **Signal confidence level**: Indicate whether your suggestion is high-confidence, medium-certainty, or exploratory to set appropriate expectations.
6. **Justify proactively**: Remember—even imperfect recommendations stimulate productive dialogue that passive summaries cannot.

The output must be a single, concise statement that defines the communication’s purpose and expected outcome.
