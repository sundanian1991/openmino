USAGE NOTES

The dot-matrix glyph is a foreground element, not a watermark. It belongs in headers, product badges, and navigation identity — never in the background.

Each dot is a filled circle with a 2:1 gap-to-dot ratio (6px dot, 3px gap). The grid is always 5 columns wide and 7 rows tall, regardless of character width.

The dot-rating variant compresses the same circle language into an inline scale. Useful for product specs, quality ratings, and category indicators.

Methodology
Performance ratings are derived from standardized lab testing (ISO 811 for waterproofing, JIS L 1099 for breathability) combined with field testing across three climate zones. Scores are normalized to 100-point scale.
Field testing was conducted in Abisko (subarctic), Chamonix (alpine), and Oman (desert) over a 12-month period. Each product was tested by a panel of 8 professional guides. Data points include comfort rating, durability assessment, and weather resistance scores.
