Beyond Scalar Rewards by Internalizing Reasoning into Score Distributions

Xin Jin, Huanqia Cai, Zhen Li, Zechao Zhan, Dengyang Jiang, Aiming Hao, Yuming Jiang, Chunle Guo, Peng Gao, Ming-Ming Cheng, Steven C.H. Hoi

Z-Image Team, Alibaba Group  ·  VCIP, CS, Nankai University

Abstract

Reward models are central to text-to-image post-training, but visual preference is subjective and better represented as a distribution over rubric scores than as a deterministic scalar. Existing scalar, score-token, and pairwise reward models over-compress uncertainty and fine-grained score differences, while reasoning-based generative rewards provide stronger judgments but are costly to deploy and difficult to use as direct optimization signals.

We propose Z-Reward, a teacher-student reward modeling framework that decouples reasoning-heavy judgment from efficient reward deployment. The teacher is a large VLM that uses reasoning to infer rubric-aligned score distributions, and is trained with Group-wise Direct Score Optimization (GDSO). The student is trained with Reasoning-Internalized Score Distillation (RISD), which transfers the teacher's reasoning-conditioned score distribution into a compact VLM without requiring explicit reasoning chains at inference time.

1. Introduction

Reward models are a key component of post-training, where they provide the preference signals used for model selection, data curation, and reward-guided optimization. Unlike mathematics or coding rewards, however, visual preferences are inherently subjective: the same generated image can receive different judgments from different annotators, especially for aesthetics, realism, and fine-grained prompt alignment.

核心张力：高质量打分需要推理和不确定性感知，而可扩展的后训练需要快速、直接、可微的奖励信号。Z-Reward 通过解耦判断质量和奖励效率来解决这个矛盾。

Scalar, score-token, and pairwise reward models compress preference into a single value or comparison, which is efficient but discards annotator uncertainty. For example, two images may both collapse to score 4 under a discrete scoring scheme, even though one is slightly below the 4-point boundary and the other is slightly above it.

Our key insight is that reward models do not need to reproduce how a teacher reasons; they need to reproduce how a reasoning teacher judges. Z-Reward resolves this tension by decoupling judgment quality from reward efficiency: instead of forcing the student to imitate the sequential process of reasoning, Z-Reward allows the compact model to internalize the teacher's reasoning-conditioned judgment directly into score distributions.

2. Annotation and Datasets

Annotation Document

We build the annotation document around four user- and production-critical dimensions: Text–Image Alignment, Realism, Aesthetics, and Physical Plausibility. Each dimension is scored with a five-level rubric that specifies how different error patterns should affect the score. Final annotations are recorded on a nine-level half-point scale, i.e., s ∈ {1.0, 1.5, …, 5.0}.

标注规模：4 维度 × 5 个整数级 × 每 bin 15-20 个标注样例 ≈ 300-400 个参考图像。标注文档在过程中持续更新，加入新发现的 corner case。

Annotation Workflow

Annotators (1) assign pointwise scores to generated candidates according to the rubric and example document, (2) compare candidates under the same prompt and within the same coarse score bin to shift distinguishable samples by ±0.5, and (3) submit the results to quality-control annotators for final review. Only data from annotators whose audited accuracy exceeds a preset threshold is admitted into the training set.

Context Mismatch

This process exposes two context mismatches: (1) the full annotation document is too long to place into a reward model's context — four dimensions, five score bins, and 15 images per bin already require approximately 4 × 5 × 15 × 1024 = 307,200 image tokens; (2) annotators can compare same-prompt candidates during score adjustment, while a deployable pointwise reward model only observes one text–image pair at a time.

3. Method

Given a prompt p, an image I, and a reward dimension d ∈ D, the teacher first generates a reasoning trace ρ and then predicts a distribution over rubric-aligned score bins s ∈ S:

qθ(s | p, I, d, ρ),   s ∈ S (1)

We treat qθ as a predictive distribution over rubric-aligned score bins, rather than as a directly observed empirical annotator distribution. Since scalable annotation usually provides one rubric-calibrated score per text–image pair, the distribution is learned implicitly from large-scale score supervision, same-prompt score-gap constraints, and the teacher's reasoning-conditioned score-token probabilities.

分布 vs 一次性标注：虽然标注者只给一个分数，但模型学到的是一个分布——捕捉了"这个分数附近还有哪些合理分数"的不确定性。这遵循 ordinal soft-label 视角：相邻分数 bin 之间应该有结构化的不确定性，而不是被当作无关类别。

The expected scalar score is obtained from the decoded distribution:

μθ(p, I, d, ρ) = Σs∈S s · qθ(s | p, I, d, ρ) (2)

3.1 Group-wise Direct Score Optimization (GDSO)

We begin from GRPO, which samples a group of responses for the same input and optimizes the policy using group-normalized advantages:

Ai = (ri − mean({rk})) / (std({rk}) + ε) (3)

GRPO alone treats the parsed score as a scalar reward. We therefore introduce GDSO, which augments policy-gradient optimization with direct supervised gradients on score distributions and score gaps. GDSO has three loss components:

POINTWISE
绝对分数校准
rpt = 1 − |μ − ŝ| / range
让预测期望分数对齐人类标注的绝对分数
PAIRWISE
分数间距监督
rpw = 1 − avg|Δμ − Δŝ| / range
不只学"谁更好"，还学"好多少"
POLICY GRAD
GRPO 优势归一化
LGRPO = −E[A · log π − β·KL]
组内归一化的优势估计驱动策略更新

The overall teacher-training objective combines all three:

LGDSO = LGRPO({rj,i}) + αpt · LptCE + αpw · Lpw (14)
为什么不用 Bradley-Terry？ BT 模型只要求赢方分数 > 输方分数，可以无限拉大间距。而 GDSO 的 pairwise loss 要求预测间距 = 标注间距，保持分数绝对校准。这对 reward model 的实际部署至关重要——如果间距被任意放大，模型的绝对打分就失去了意义。

3.2 Reasoning-Internalized Score Distillation (RISD)

After teacher training, the student model is trained to predict the teacher's distribution directly, without generating the teacher's reasoning chain:

LRISD = E(p,I,d)[ DKL( qT(s|p,I,d,ρT) ∥ qϕ(s|p,I,d) ) ] (15)
蒸馏的是判断，不是推理：OPD 让 Student 模仿 Teacher 的推理轨迹（~750 tokens），但 9B Student 自身推理能力弱，探索不到好的轨迹。RISD 直接学 Teacher 的判断结果（分数分布），绕过了"推理能力瓶颈"。结果：1 token vs ~750 tokens，HPA 88.6% vs 83.1%。

5. Validating Z-Reward as an Optimizable Reward Signal

We apply Z-Reward to the RL stage of text-to-image generation. The student reward model predicts score distributions for four dimensions — text–image alignment, realism, aesthetics, and physical plausibility — and these gradients are backpropagated through the denoising process to update the generator.

Compared with the strong SFT baseline, the model optimized using Z-Reward achieves a net GSB improvement of 41.3%. This confirms that the improvements measured by the reward model are reflected in human judgments.

6. Discussion

Reasoning-Score Coupling

One limitation is that the direct SFT-style losses may occasionally make the final score depend more on direct score supervision than on the generated reasoning trace itself. Future work could add explicit reasoning-score consistency checks.

Potential Generalization

Although this paper focuses on reward modeling for text-to-image generation, the teacher-student framework and RISD distillation strategy generalize to any sequence-to-score task where reasoning improves scoring quality but deployment requires efficiency.
