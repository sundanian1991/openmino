Abstract

Reward models are central to text-to-image post-training, but visual preference is subjective and better represented as a distribution over rubric scores than as a deterministic scalar. Existing scalar, score-token, and pairwise reward models over-compress uncertainty, while reasoning-based generative rewards are costly to deploy.

We propose Z-Reward, a teacher-student framework that decouples reasoning-heavy judgment from efficient reward deployment. The teacher uses GDSO to learn score distributions; the student uses RISD to internalize these distributions without explicit reasoning chains at inference time.

3. Method

Given a prompt p, image I, and reward dimension d, the teacher generates a reasoning trace ρ and predicts a distribution over score bins:

qθ(s | p, I, d, ρ),   s ∈ S (1)

The expected scalar score is the distribution's expectation:

μθ(p, I, d, ρ) = Σs∈S s · qθ(s | p, I, d, ρ) (2)

3.1 Group-wise Direct Score Optimization (GDSO)

GDSO augments GRPO with direct supervised gradients on score distributions and score gaps. The overall objective:

LGDSO = LGRPO({rj,i}) + αpt · LptCE + αpw · Lpw (14)
POINTWISE
绝对分数校准
rpt = 1 − |μ − ŝ| / range
让预测期望分数对齐人类标注的绝对分数
不只是相对排序，而是绝对校准
PAIRWISE
分数间距监督
rpw = 1 − avg|Δμ − Δŝ| / range
不只学"谁更好"，还学"好多少"
比 BT 更精细：要求间距 = 标注间距
POLICY GRAD
GRPO 优势归一化
Ai = (ri − mean) / (std + ε)
组内归一化的优势估计驱动策略更新
策略梯度 + KL 正则化
为什么不用 Bradley-Terry？ BT 只要求赢方分数 > 输方，可以无限拉大间距。GDSO 的 pairwise loss 要求预测间距 = 标注间距，保持分数绝对校准。如果间距被任意放大，模型的绝对打分就失去了意义。

3.2 Reasoning-Internalized Score Distillation (RISD)

The student is trained to predict the teacher's distribution directly, without generating reasoning chains:

LRISD = E(p,I,d)[ DKL( qT(s|p,I,d,ρT) ∥ qϕ(s|p,I,d) ) ] (15)
OPD (BASELINE)
模仿推理轨迹
  • Student 采样自己的推理链
  • Teacher 提供 token 级 log-prob
  • ~750 tokens 输出
  • 9B 推理能力弱，探索不到好轨迹

匹配分数分布

  • 不学推理过程
  • 1 token 输出

6. Discussion

Reasoning-Score Coupling

The direct SFT-style losses may occasionally make the final score depend more on direct score supervision than on the generated reasoning trace. Future work could add explicit reasoning-score consistency checks.

Potential Generalization

The teacher-student framework and RISD distillation strategy generalize to any sequence-to-score task where reasoning improves scoring quality but deployment requires efficiency.
