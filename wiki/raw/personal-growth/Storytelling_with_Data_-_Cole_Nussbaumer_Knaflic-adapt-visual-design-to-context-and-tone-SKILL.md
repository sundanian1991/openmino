# Adapt Visual Design to Context and Tone

Apply this skill after core visual clarity is achieved, to align your communication with delivery format, emotional intent, and external constraints.

## When to Use
- Choosing between live presentation, email, report, or dashboard
- Branding requires non-standard backgrounds (e.g., dark themes)
- The message requires a specific emotional resonance (urgent, celebratory, sober)
- You must avoid creating ineffective “sliduments” (hybrid slide-documents)

## How to Execute
1. **Adapt to communication mechanism**:
   - **Live presentations**: Keep slides sparse; use visuals to reinforce spoken points
   - **Written documents/emails**: Include more detail; ensure self-containment
   - **Sliduments (if unavoidable)**: Use appendix slides for written-detail needs
2. **Set intentional tone**:
   - Choose tone early (e.g., urgent, analytical, inspirational)
   - Let tone guide color palette, language, and emphasis style
   - Ensure consistency—playful graphics undermine serious topics
3. **Handle dark backgrounds**:
   - Reverse prominence logic: light colors (white, yellow) now stand out
   - Use high-contrast, saturated colors that work against black
   - Test readability and avoid “heavy” visual tone unless brand-mandated
4. **Validate alignment**: Does the design match audience expectations, format constraints, and message gravity?

> **Note**: Tone and format adaptations should never compromise core clarity or accessibility.
