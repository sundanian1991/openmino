# Define Audience-Specific Communication Context

## When to Use
Use this skill at the start of any data communication task—including presentations, written reports, or data storytelling—if you have not yet defined a specific, actionable audience. Do not proceed with content creation until this step is complete.

## How to Execute
1. **Reject generic labels**: Replace broad terms like "internal and external stakeholders" with concrete roles or groups (e.g., "school board members," "product managers").
2. **Identify the primary decision maker**: Pinpoint the individual or group with authority to act on your recommendation.
3. **Segment if needed**: If multiple audience types have divergent needs, plan separate communications rather than compromising clarity.
4. **Assess relationship and trust**: Determine whether your audience views you as credible ('established'), neutral ('neutral'), or skeptical ('needs proof').
5. **Shape narrative choices**: Use your audience profile to guide tone (formal vs. conversational), detail depth (executive summary vs. technical appendix), and emphasis (ROI vs. user experience).

> **Output**: A clearly defined audience profile that informs all subsequent design and messaging decisions.
