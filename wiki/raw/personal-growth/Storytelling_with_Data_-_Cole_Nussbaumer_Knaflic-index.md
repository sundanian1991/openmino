# Data Storytelling & Visualization Skills Collection  
*From the book "28398A58-B095-446C-Acc1-25Bc0B45C48C"*

This collection distills 30 essential skills for crafting compelling, ethical, and effective data visualizations and narratives. Covering foundational design principles, audience-centered communication strategies, cognitive psychology insights, and practical techniques for chart selection and refinement, these skills empower analysts, designers, and decision-makers to transform raw data into clear, actionable stories. Whether you're building dashboards, presenting findings, or developing organizational competency in data storytelling, this guide provides a structured path toward visual clarity and impact.

## Available Skills

| Skill | Description | Use When |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| [avoid-secondary-y-axes-and-3d-visuals](avoid-secondary-y-axes-and-3d-visuals/SKILL.md) | Replaces misleading 3D charts and dual-axis time-series visuals with clearer, more honest alternatives. | When tempted to use 3D effects for single-dimension data or a secondary y-axis to show two metrics with different units or scales—common in multi-metric dashboards, business performance reports, or time-series comparisons. |
| [construct-heatmaps-for-tabular-pattern-recognition](construct-heatmaps-for-tabular-pattern-recognition/SKILL.md) | Converts numerical tabular data into a heatmap to help viewers instantly identify high/low values and underlying patterns. | When you have a grid of numbers (e.g., performance matrices, survey results, or cross-tabulated data) and need to reduce mental scanning effort by leveraging color as a visual cue. |
| [logical-ordering-categorical-data](logical-ordering-categorical-data/SKILL.md) | Orders categories in visualizations based on analytical intent—such as satisfaction, dissatisfaction, or usage—when the data lacks intrinsic order. | When displaying non-ordinal categorical data and you need to align visual sequence with your primary story without increasing cognitive load. |
| [spaghetti-graph-deconstruction](spaghetti-graph-deconstruction/SKILL.md) | Reduces visual clutter in multi-series line charts ("spaghetti graphs") by applying structured deconstruction strategies. | When a time-series line chart contains excessive overlapping data series that obscure trends or comparisons, and the audience needs clearer insight into specific patterns, cross-category differences, or contextual relationships. |
| [strategic-ordering-in-stacked-bar-charts](strategic-ordering-in-stacked-bar-charts/SKILL.md) | Orders segments in stacked bar charts to maximize interpretability of key trends when displaying ordinal categorical data over time. | When one segment is the primary focus and categories have a natural order (e.g., performance levels: Miss < Meet < Exceed). |
| [effective-data-visualization-tool-learning](effective-data-visualization-tool-learning/SKILL.md) | Learn and master data visualization tools through focused, hands-on experimentation. | When starting a new visualization tool (e.g., Excel, Tableau, Python) or when struggling to achieve a specific visual outcome in your current tool. |
| [minimalist-table-design](minimalist-table-design/SKILL.md) | Applies minimalist formatting principles to data tables to enhance legibility and reduce visual distraction. | When presenting tabular data that requires immediate legibility and reduced visual distraction—especially when units vary across columns or when audiences need to scan rows accurately. |
| [articulate-action-oriented-objectives](articulate-action-oriented-objectives/SKILL.md) | Formulates clear, specific calls to action for business communications based on data insights. | When presenting findings to business professionals, analysts, or managers and you need to move the audience toward a decision or concrete next step—especially when the communication risks ending with passive observations. |
| [integrate-supporting-and-opposing-data-ethically](integrate-supporting-and-opposing-data-ethically/SKILL.md) | Ensures data-driven narratives include relevant counter-evidence and contextual information to maintain credibility and intellectual honesty. | When constructing a data-based argument for external or decision-making audiences—especially when omitting contradictory evidence could mislead or erode trust. |
| [pie-chart-alternatives-for-proportional-change](pie-chart-alternatives-for-proportional-change/SKILL.md) | Selects and implements optimal visualization alternatives to dual pie charts for communicating proportional change between two states. | When you need to show part-to-whole relationships or distribution shifts across two time points or conditions, especially in survey reporting. |
| [avoid-pie-and-donut-charts](avoid-pie-and-donut-charts/SKILL.md) | Replaces pie and donut charts with horizontal bar charts for proportional or part-to-whole data in professional contexts. | When considering a pie or donut chart to display market share, category proportions, or any data that sums to 100%. |
| [effective-axis-labeling-for-time-series](effective-axis-labeling-for-time-series/SKILL.md) | Applies strategic axis labeling to time-series charts to communicate overall trends rather than exact daily values. | When your audience prioritizes interpretability and cognitive ease over granular date lookup or precise daily metrics. |
| [define-audience-specific-communication-context](define-audience-specific-communication-context/SKILL.md) | Defines a precise audience profile by identifying the primary decision maker, assessing trust levels, and tailoring messaging accordingly. | When creating presentations, reports, or data stories to ensure relevance and impact—especially when the initial audience definition is vague (e.g., 'all stakeholders'). |
| [time-budgeting-for-data-storytelling](time-budgeting-for-data-storytelling/SKILL.md) | Allocates dedicated time for refining data communication after analysis is complete. | When concluding an analytical project and preparing to present findings to an external or decision-making audience—especially when default visualizations are insufficient for clear, persuasive storytelling. |
| [build-organizational-data-storytelling-competency](build-organizational-data-storytelling-competency/SKILL.md) | Implements a multi-tiered capability-building strategy to embed data storytelling skills across teams or departments. | When an organization aims to systematically improve how it communicates insights through data visuals and narratives, especially when leadership support and training resources are available. |
| [animated-vs-static-data-presentation](animated-vs-static-data-presentation/SKILL.md) | Creates two complementary versions of time-series visualizations—one animated for live storytelling and one fully annotated static version for circulation. | When delivering a live presentation with follow-up distribution needs, a clear narrative arc exists, and the audience will experience the content in both real-time and asynchronous formats. |
| [paper-sketching-for-data-ideation](paper-sketching-for-data-ideation/SKILL.md) | Uses rapid, unconstrained paper sketching to explore and validate visual approaches for data storytelling early in the design process. | During initial ideation phases for slide layouts, narrative flows, or data visualizations—especially when facing creative blocks or ambiguity. |
| [seeking-and-applying-external-feedback](seeking-and-applying-external-feedback/SKILL.md) | Validates whether a data visualization effectively communicates its intended message by gathering unbiased feedback from a representative audience. | When uncertain if your visual clearly conveys the core insight or when preparing high-stakes deliverables that require audience alignment. |
| [context-driven-data-storytelling](context-driven-data-storytelling/SKILL.md) | Applies a unified, sequential framework to create effective data visualizations by first establishing audience context and a clear “Big Idea,” then executing six core storytelling lessons. | When preparing data communications (e.g., business presentations, reports, dashboards) intended to inform decisions or drive action. |
| [enforce-zero-baseline-bar-charts](enforce-zero-baseline-bar-charts/SKILL.md) | Ensures bar charts use a zero baseline on the value axis to prevent misleading visual comparisons. | When creating or reviewing bar charts that compare magnitudes between categories—especially when data is represented as bar lengths. |
| [strategic-visual-emphasis-using-preattentive-attributes](strategic-visual-emphasis-using-preattentive-attributes/SKILL.md) | Guides audience attention to a single key insight by applying preattentive attributes to ≤10% of elements while de-emphasizing the rest. | When you have a clear primary message and need it to be instantly noticeable without cognitive competition. |
| [positive-negative-stacked-bar-design](positive-negative-stacked-bar-design/SKILL.md) | Creates a stacked bar chart that visualizes net change by combining positive and negative components around a zero baseline, with a highlighted gap representing unmet need. | When you need to show how inflows and outflows contribute to a final shortfall or surplus—especially in workforce planning, gap analysis, or net-change scenarios where the gap itself is the key insight. |
| [adapt-visual-design-to-context-and-tone](adapt-visual-design-to-context-and-tone/SKILL.md) | Adjusts data visualization design based on delivery mechanism, intended emotional tone, and branding constraints like dark backgrounds. | When finalizing visuals for specific audiences, formats, or organizational requirements. |
| [apply-gestalt-and-alignment-principles-for-clarity](apply-gestalt-and-alignment-principles-for-clarity/SKILL.md) | Structures visual layouts using Gestalt grouping principles and intentional alignment/white space to reduce clutter and guide attention. | When a visual feels disorganized, contains multiple elements, and needs to convey relationships without explicit lines or borders. |
| [design-data-visualizations-by-function-and-form](design-data-visualizations-by-function-and-form/SKILL.md) | Selects and structures data visualizations based on a clearly defined functional goal and distinguishes explanatory from exploratory analysis. | When creating visuals to drive audience understanding or action, not during data discovery. |
| [three-act-data-storytelling](three-act-data-storytelling/SKILL.md) | Structures data-driven communications using a three-act narrative framework to inspire action or decisions. | When you have a clear audience, a defined desired action, and a data-backed insight that requires persuasive storytelling—not for exploratory or reference-only documents. |
| [minimize-cognitive-load-in-visual-communications](minimize-cognitive-load-in-visual-communications/SKILL.md) | Reduces mental effort required by audiences to process visual communications by eliminating extraneous elements and using explicit, accessible text. | When designing visuals for mixed or non-expert audiences where clarity and immediate comprehension are critical. |
| [distinguish-forecast-from-actual-data](distinguish-forecast-from-actual-data/SKILL.md) | Applies distinct visual treatments to separate historical (actual) data from forward-looking (forecast) data in time-series charts. | When a visualization includes both actual and forecast values and you need to clearly communicate uncertainty to viewers. |
| [iterate-visual-design-using-optometrist-approach](iterate-visual-design-using-optometrist-approach/SKILL.md) | Refines data visualizations through side-by-side comparison of incremental changes, selecting the version that best supports the message. | When unsure which design choice improves clarity, and you have time to iterate. |
| [horizontal-stacked-bar-survey-priority](horizontal-stacked-bar-survey-priority/SKILL.md) | Creates a horizontal stacked bar chart to visualize survey data where respondents ranked items by priority, showing both total importance and rank composition. | When you need to communicate aggregate preference strength alongside how that preference is distributed across ranking tiers. |
