# Seeking and Applying External Feedback

## When to Use
Use this skill when:
- You have a working draft of a visualization, slide, or report
- You are unsure whether your audience will interpret it as intended
- You need to validate clarity before finalizing or presenting
- The content is not highly sensitive (or has been anonymized)

## How to Execute
1. Identify a colleague, peer, or team member who represents your target audience or possesses general analytical literacy.
2. Present your visual **without** explaining your intent or guiding their interpretation.
3. Ask them to verbalize their real-time thought process using these standard prompts:
   - "What do you notice first?"
   - "What observations or conclusions are you drawing?"
   - "What questions does this raise for you?"
   - "What would help make the main point clearer?"
4. Listen without defending or correcting—capture all feedback verbatim.
5. Analyze responses to assess alignment with your intended message:
   - If they focus on the right elements and reach correct conclusions → your visual is effective
   - If attention is misdirected or confusion arises → identify specific elements causing issues
6. Use these insights to guide your next iteration (e.g., adjust color emphasis, simplify labels, reposition key data).
7. Repeat the feedback loop after implementing changes if significant uncertainty remains.

## Key Variables
- `intended_message`: The core insight or action you want the audience to grasp
- `feedback_questions`: Standard prompts to elicit unbiased user interpretation

## Output
An alert containing a gap analysis between your intended message and the audience’s interpretation, highlighting required visual or textual adjustments.
