# Context-Driven Data Storytelling

Follow this end-to-end process when creating any data communication meant to influence decisions:

## Phase 1: Establish Context and Big Idea

1. **Identify the WHO**: Determine the specific audience (e.g., VP of Product) whose decisions your visualization should influence.
2. **Define the WHAT**: Articulate precisely what the audience needs to know or do as a result.
3. **Specify the HOW**: Choose the exact data and metrics that support your message.
4. **Craft the Big Idea**: Synthesize the above into one actionable sentence combining insight and recommendation.
5. **Validate alignment**: Ensure all design choices will directly serve this Big Idea.

> ⚠️ Do not proceed to visualization design without completing this phase.

## Phase 2: Execute Six Core Data Storytelling Lessons

Apply these steps sequentially—each depends on the prior:

1. **Understand the context**: Confirm audience, objective, mechanism, and tone before touching data.
2. **Choose an appropriate visual display**: Select chart type based on message (e.g., bar for comparison, line for trend); avoid pie charts and 3D.
3. **Eliminate clutter**: Remove non-essential elements (gridlines, borders, redundant labels) using Gestalt principles.
4. **Focus attention**: Use preattentive attributes (color, size, position) to guide the eye to the key message.
5. **Think like a designer**: Prioritize function over form; ensure accessibility and intuitive affordances.
6. **Tell a story**: Structure with clear beginning (context), middle (evidence), and end (action); use narrative flow and repetition.

## When to Use This Skill

- You are creating a data visualization, business presentation, or analytical report.
- Your goal is to inform a decision, recommend an action, or communicate a clear insight.
- You have defined both your audience and your communication objective.

Do not use if data or audience is undefined—this framework requires both.
