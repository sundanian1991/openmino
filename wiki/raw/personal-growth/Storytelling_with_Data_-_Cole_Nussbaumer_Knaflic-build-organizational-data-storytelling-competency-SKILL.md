# Build Organizational Data Storytelling Competency

Use this skill when your team or organization seeks to move beyond basic charting toward intentional, audience-focused data communication. This is appropriate for groups of 3+ people where role specialization is feasible and leadership recognizes communication as a critical analytical step.

## Execute the Four-Pillar Strategy

### 1. Implement Foundational Training for All Relevant Staff
- Host a "Storytelling with Data" book club: read and discuss one chapter weekly, applying concepts to real work examples.
- Run DIY workshops using actual team deliverables for critique and visual makeovers.
- Launch "Makeover Monday": issue weekly challenges to redesign ineffective visuals using core principles.
- Establish a structured feedback loop requiring draft sharing and peer critiques.
- Run recognition contests (e.g., "And the winner is…") to showcase and reward effective data stories.

### 2. Invest in 1–2 Internal Experts
- Identify staff with interest or aptitude in data visualization.
- Provide dedicated resources: books, courses, coaching, and protected practice time.
- Formalize their role as "data viz consultants" to support peers.
- Enable knowledge sharing via brown-bag sessions or office hours.

### 3. Use Outsourcing Strategically
- Engage external experts only for high-stakes or time-sensitive deliverables.
- Require knowledge transfer during engagements (e.g., co-creation workshops).
- Repurpose final outputs as reusable internal templates.

### 4. Monitor and Adjust
- Track adoption through example galleries and contest participation.
- Reinforce consistent language and frameworks from training in daily feedback.
- Publicly celebrate progress to sustain momentum and cultural change.

> **Note**: Do not apply this skill to teams smaller than three people, where role specialization is impractical.
