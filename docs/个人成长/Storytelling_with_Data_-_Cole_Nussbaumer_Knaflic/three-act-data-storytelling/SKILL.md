# Three-Act Data Storytelling

Apply this skill to transform analytical content into a compelling narrative that motivates your audience to act. Follow the three-act structure below:

## Act 1: Beginning (Setup)
- Introduce the setting: when and where the issue exists.
- Frame the audience as the protagonist—identify who drives the action.
- Establish the imbalance: explain what has changed or why action is needed.
- Define the desired balance: describe the ideal future state.
- Preview your solution: briefly state how change will happen.
- Answer the audience’s implicit question: “Why should I care? What’s in it for me?”

## Act 2: Middle (Conflict)
- Develop the problem with evidence:
  - Provide relevant background.
  - Include external comparisons or benchmarks.
  - Use illustrative examples.
  - Present data that demonstrates the issue.
  - Highlight consequences of inaction.
- Evaluate possible options and build a case for your recommended solution:
  - Articulate tangible benefits.
  - Explain why the audience is uniquely positioned to act.
- Keep audience motivations (e.g., profit, competitive advantage, innovation) central throughout.

## Act 3: End (Resolution)
- Issue a clear, specific call to action: state exactly what you want the audience to do.
- Recap the problem and urgency, tying back to Act 1.
- Reiterate the required decision or next step.
- Optionally apply the “Bing, Bang, Bongo” technique:
  - **Bing**: Tell them what you’ll tell them (executive summary).
  - **Bang**: Tell them (core narrative with evidence).
  - **Bongo**: Tell them what you told them (recap focused on action).

## Integration Guidelines
- Embed data visuals that align with each narrative beat.
- Always make the “so what?” explicit in words—don’t assume the audience will infer meaning from charts alone.

Use this skill whenever your goal is to move stakeholders from understanding to action using data.
