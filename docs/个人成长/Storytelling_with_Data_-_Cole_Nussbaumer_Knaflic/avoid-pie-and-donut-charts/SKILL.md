# Avoid Pie and Donut Charts

## When to Use This Skill
Use this skill whenever you are planning to create a pie chart or donut chart to show:
- Market share displays
- Proportional data
- Part-to-whole relationships

This applies especially in business, academic, or professional reporting where clarity and accurate comparison matter.

## How to Execute
1. **Reject the pie/donut chart** by default—do not use it unless all of the following rare conditions are met:
   - Only 2–3 categories exist
   - One segment dominates (>70%) and that dominance is the sole message
   - You can justify why no other visualization works

2. **Choose a horizontal bar chart** as the preferred alternative:
   - Sort bars logically (e.g., largest to smallest)
   - Ensure all bars share a common baseline for easy endpoint comparison
   - Add a footnote stating "Total = 100%" if part-to-whole context is essential

3. **Never apply 3D effects**—they distort segment sizes and worsen readability.

4. **Validate your choice**: Confirm that the new chart enables viewers to accurately compare magnitudes without relying on angle or arc-length estimation.

> **Default Action**: Replace any proposed pie or donut chart with a sorted horizontal bar chart.
