# Integrate Supporting and Opposing Data Ethically

## When to Use
Use this skill when creating:
- Final data narratives
- Stakeholder reports
- Strategic recommendations

Do **not** use for internal brainstorming, early drafts, or exploratory analysis where full context isn’t yet required.

## How to Execute
1. **Identify all relevant evidence types**: Classify your data as *supporting*, *opposing*, or *contextual*.
2. **Assess disclosure needs**: Determine how much counter-evidence to include based on:
   - Situation sensitivity (high-stakes decisions require more transparency)
   - Existing trust with your audience
   - Audience expertise (experts expect nuanced treatment of limitations)
3. **Integrate opposing/contextual data**: Explicitly acknowledge limitations, alternative interpretations, or contradictory findings—even if you ultimately argue against them.
4. **Avoid one-sided presentation**: Never present only data that supports your conclusion while omitting known contradictory or contextual information.
5. **Demonstrate intellectual honesty**: Frame counterpoints not as weaknesses but as evidence of thorough analysis.

> **Output Alert**: If your communication omits relevant contradictory or contextual data, flag it as a potential ethics and credibility risk.
