# Paper Sketching for Data Ideation

Use this skill when you are:
- Early in a data visualization or presentation design process
- Unsure which visual format best communicates your message
- Working with blank paper or a whiteboard (not digital tools)
- Seeking to overcome creative blocks through low-stakes exploration

## Execution Steps

1. **Gather materials**: Use blank paper or a whiteboard—avoid digital tools at this stage.
2. **Generate alternatives**: Sketch multiple visual approaches side-by-side (e.g., bar chart vs. slopegraph vs. annotated table).
3. **Prioritize structure over polish**: Focus on layout, information hierarchy, and narrative flow—not precision, color, or aesthetics.
4. **Leverage psychological freedom**: Remember that paper sketches are easy to discard, reducing emotional attachment to weak ideas.
5. **Break creative blocks**: Draw freely—"there is something freeing about drawing on blank paper."
6. **Evaluate feasibility**: Once a promising concept emerges, assess whether it can be implemented with your available tools and expertise.
7. **Transition digitally**: Translate the validated sketch into digital form using appropriate software (e.g., Excel, PowerPoint, Figma).

> **Note**: Do not use this skill when replicating known templates or working under strict brand guidelines—it’s designed for open-ended exploration, not execution of predefined formats.
